## 🌱 Hypothesis: Iboga and Restorative Epigenetics in Autistic Phenotypes

## Background

A mouse model study by Judit Biosca-Brull et al. (2024) showed that four genes demonstrated a substantial increase in expression following the administration of ibogaine (oxytocin (Oxt), vasopressin (Avp), cerebellin (Cbln) 2 and 4 precursors)[1](#ref1). Notably, these gene pathways have been consistently shown to be downregulated in autism [2](#ref2).

## Ideas
- We could study the reopening of critical periods that psychedelics induce. One study found that iboga induced the longest reopening of critical period (4 weeks) compared to other common psychedelics. I am curious to map novel epigenetic biomarkers related to neoteny and critical period development using tools such as Dr. Matteo Pellegrini's Epigenetic Pacemaker.

## Future Directions

- Mapping the epigenomic shifts of neural organoids and iPSC neural crest cell cultures before and after iboga alkaloid introduction (short- and long-term) using tools such as Dr. Matteo Pellegrini's Epigenetic Pacemaker to track how DNA methylation changes as a function of time-dependent epigenetic states
